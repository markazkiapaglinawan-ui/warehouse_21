CHAPTER I INTRODUCTION

Rationale of the Study
      A Car Rental is a service that temporarily rents or provides flexible transportation options to the individuals, like an automobile, for a specific period of time. Access to reliable vehicles remains one of the most important needs in communities, yet many customers face challenges in navigating rental processes. Customers are often forced to find rental vehicles on different social media platforms, such as posts, groups, and pages on Facebook or in Facebook Marketplace. Additionally, customers can also find Airbnb for cars which leads to consuming valuable time, as many of the vehicles they try to rent are not suitable for their preferences or unavailable on the dates they require. As a result, customers must repeatedly search for alternatives, leading to frustration and inefficiency.
       Previously, the traditional method of managing car rentals took hours of visits to the car rental counters or calls, and it was time consuming and inconvenient for customers. Also, using manual record-keeping is prone to errors and inefficiency. This approach encourages community-driven rental transactions, offering flexibility for both car owners who earn extra income by renting out idle cars and renters who gain access to affordable vehicle options. According to Archive (2020), having a web-based booking system in car rentals provides a seamless workflow in the booking process and contributes to greater customer satisfaction to the customer and operational effectiveness in the vehicle rental business.

      The proposed Rent a Car System aims to provide customers with a seamless and personalized user experience, featuring a wide range of vehicle options and a secure, efficient rental process (Nobel, 2023). It also offers convenience, flexibility, and affordability to customers. By integrating customer information with a directory of available cars and their categories, such as sedan, SUV, and van, the system ensures that individuals or customers are directed to the right vehicle without confusion or delay. It reduces the risk of double bookings and unnecessary waiting times.
      Furthermore, the Rent a Car System integrates users who can act as owners and renters. Owners can submit a vehicle to the system that they want to rent while also being a renter who can book a vehicle. It also integrates admin users, allowing them to manage car approvals and view system-wide reports. With these features, it improves efficiency and customer satisfaction across the entire rental process with the help of the technology system, and it also modernizes transportation services.

Related Literature

      A car rental system is designed to provide users with an efficient and convenient platform for renting vehicles through a computerized process. It allows customers to browse available cars, make reservations, and manage rental transactions in an organized and automated manner, reducing the need for manual processes and in-person transactions (Yadav et al., 2021). In another study, a web-based online car rental system improves accessibility by allowing users to book vehicles anytime and anywhere through an internet-based platform, reducing the limitations of traditional rental methods and enhancing customer convenience (Singh et al., 2020). Similarly, the development of web-based car rental management systems highlights the importance of automation in handling rental operations including booking, vehicle tracking, and transaction management (Ogbiti et al., 2024).

      The SCREMS - Smart Car Rental Management System shows that developing a smart and automated car rental platform improves the overall efficiency of vehicle rental operations by integrating features such as online booking, vehicle management, and user authentication within a centralized system (Aldinovandi, 2025). The system enhances user convenience by allowing customers to easily access available vehicles, make reservations, and manage rental transactions in a more organized and automated process  compared  to  traditional  manual  methods.  The  study  emphasizes  that

implementing a smart rental management system improves operational accuracy, reduces human errors, and provides better control for administrators in managing vehicle inventory and rental records

      The Rental Car Management System emphasizes that developing a computerized car rental platform significantly improves the efficiency of managing vehicle rentals by automating key processes such as user registration, vehicle selection, booking management, and administrative control (Keeriti & Kumar, 2024). The system enhances customer convenience by allowing users to easily browse available vehicles based on preferences and availability, while also ensuring secure transactions and organized record management. In addition, the implementation of features such as real-time vehicle tracking, automated maintenance reminders, and billing management improves overall operational efficiency and reduces manual workload. It highlights that integrating reporting and analytics tools supports better decision-making for administrators and enhances service quality, resulting in improved user satisfaction and more efficient rental operations.

      The Decision Support System for pricing in car rental companies emphasizes that rental firms need accurate and competitive pricing strategies to improve profitability and market performance. The research found factors such as market growth, operational risks, and changing business conditions require car rental to adopt systems that can support cost estimation and pricing decisions using data-driven approaches (Cunha &

de Souza, 2015). Similarly, the development of a Vehicle Management System highlights that traditional manual processes in managing vehicle rental often lead to issues such as inaccurate records, long processing time reports, incorrect vehicle bookings. To address these problems, the study implemented a computerized membership-based system using a relational database to improve data organization, accuracy, and efficiency in rental operations (Ahmed et al., 2019).

      The study on an Online Car Rental System using web technology highlights that implementing a web-based platform significantly improves the accessibility and efficiency of a car rental service by allowing customers to browse available vehicles, view description and price, and make reservations anytime and anywhere through multiple devices (Mohite et al., 2022). The system also enhances user experience by providing essential functionalities such as user registration, login, and rental management, while enabling administrators to efficiently manage car listings, update pricings, and control rental operations through centralized systems. Similarly, another web-based car rental business management system emphasizes that integrating booking functions with centralized data management improves transaction accuracy, reduces risks associated with manual booking processes, and provides better protection for both customers and car owners by ensuring proper record tracking and secure data handling (Rahman & Rusli, 2023). Furthermore, an online rental system concludes that automating the rental process through a computerized system improves operational efficiency and eliminates the limitations of manual procedure by allowing customers to

book vehicle based on their requirements, including vehicle type and rental duration, resulting in a more convenient and user-friendly rental experience (Archana, 2019).

CHAPTER 3 METHODOLOGY
      This chapter outlines the systematic processes and technical frameworks utilized to build a Rent a Car System. It covers the development environment, the feasibility of the architecture, and the specific design to development pipeline used to transition from Figma wireframes to a functional Java Swing application.


Environment

      The Rent a Car System was developed in a controlled academic environment using desktop-based tools. The system utilizes Java programming with Java Swing for graphical user interface (GUI) and MySQL for database management. Development was conducted using an integrated development environment (IDE) such as NetBeans, ensuring efficient coding, debugging, and testing.

Locale

      The system is intended for use within the local community, particularly in Cebu City. It focuses on connecting local car owners and renters within the same geographic area. The system is designed to support local rental practices and pricing.
Development Setting

      The system was engineered within the College of Computer, Information and Communication Technology (CCICT) at Cebu Technological University - Main

Campus. This environment provided the necessary high-speed network and workstation stability required for intensive Java compilation and database testing and peer review.

Given the team's proficiency in object-oriented programming and database management, the project's technical requirements are well-aligned with the research team's capabilities, ensuring a smooth transition from conceptualization to development.

CHAPTER 4 CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATION



      The development and evaluation of the proposed Rent a Car System demonstrate that the study has successfully achieved its primary objective, which is the design and implementation of a desktop-based car rental management system. Through the utilization of Java programming language and MySQL database management, the researchers were able to transform traditional, manual, and fragmented car rental processes into a centralized and automated system that improves efficiency, organization, and accessibility.
      The system architecture, grounded in Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) principles, ensures modularity, scalability, and maintainability, allowing for future enhancements and system expansion. The implemented core functionalities, including role-based authentication, dynamic vehicle listing, and comprehensive booking management, significantly improve operational workflow and reduce inefficiencies commonly observed in conventional rental systems.
      In comparison with existing platforms such as ANIS Transport and Facebook Marketplace, the proposed system provides a more specialized and structured solution by integrating a dual-role feature that allows users to function as both car owners and

renters within a unified platform. This feature enhances system flexibility and improves user engagement by supporting multiple rental roles in a single environment.
      Despite its successful implementation as a functional prototype, the system is subject to several limitations. These include the absence of real-time GPS tracking, reliance on simulated payment processing, and the lack of automated legal contract generation. As such, while the system is suitable for academic and proof-of-concept purposes, further refinement is necessary to meet the requirements of full-scale commercial deployment.
      Overall, the Rent a Car System presents a viable foundation for digitalizing vehicle rental services and demonstrates the practical application of software engineering principles in solving real-world problems. With future enhancements, the system has the potential to evolve into a more comprehensive and industry-ready rental management platform.


Recommendations

      To transition the Rent a Car System from an academic prototype to an industry-ready solution, the researchers propose the following strategic enhancements:
Add Real GPS Tracking: To make the system safer, we suggest adding real-time GPS tracking so car owners can monitor their vehicles at any time, which builds trust for both parties.

Integrate Real Payment Gateways: Our current system uses simulated payments for testing. Adding real online payment methods (like GCash, Maya, or bank transfers) is the next logical step to make the system ready for actual business transactions.
Create Digital Contracts: To make every rental official, we suggest adding a feature that automatically generates rental contracts that users can sign electronically, saving everyone time and paperwork.
Enable Real Subscription & Membership System: Since the membership upgrade is currently for simulation only, future improvements should connect it to real subscription services, allowing users to actually upgrade from Free to Pro accounts and access premium features with real payments.
Move to Mobile: Right now, the system only works on desktop computers. Moving it to a mobile app would make it much more convenient for users to book cars while they are on the go.
Add Smart Features: Future updates could include AI suggestions to help people find the right car faster, or dynamic pricing that adjusts rates based on demand.
Improve Admin Tools & Security: We also recommend strengthening security for user data and creating a more detailed dashboard for admins to track everything happening in the system in real-time.

Add Offline Functionality: Since the system currently relies on an online database, future improvements may include limited offline capabilities, allowing users to access basic features even without internet connection and sync data once reconnected.
      Overall, these steps will help turn our project from a school requirement into a competitive and professional car rental solution.

Authentication

      The system employs a role-based authentication model specifically designed to separate the peer-to-Peer (P2P) rental functions from administrative management. General users can securely log in to browse available vehicles and manage bookings, or utilize the registration module to join the platform, with all credentials verified against

the MySQL database. Conversely, the administrative workflow provides a restricted, high-level interface that allows for the oversight of car inventory and user accounts, ensuring that the Java Swing dashboard remains secure and that management operations are isolated from the standard user experience.

Figure 13: Program workflow for Users



Figure 14: Flowchart for Users



Users

The user workflow initiates upon a successful login, directing the client to a

centralized dashboard that serves as the hub for all peer-to-peer (P2P) rental activities. The primary function facilitates car bookings, where users can browse various vehicle categories or act as a car owner by listing their own vehicles to earn revenue, including an option to assign a driver. All coordination occurs within the inbox, where renters can edit or cancel requests and owners can confirm or withdraw bookings. To differentiate

the experience, the system implements a tiered membership: Free users are limited to

5 car listings, while Pro users enjoy unlimited listings, priority placement in search results, and access to a dedicated analytics module to track earnings and performance. This is supported by a notification center that issues digital receipts for successful transactions and alerts for maintenance or help & support ticket responses. Finally, the Account Management module ensures users can update their profile and driver credentials to maintain system compliance.



Figure 15: Program workflow of Admin

Administrator

      The administrative workflow serves as the central platform command center, granting unrestricted access to the admin dashboard, which is segmented into five core management domains. Through the Verification module, the admin presiding over the

car listing pipeline can approve, reject, or request edits for vehicle submissions while also managing user upgrades and identity verification to ensure platform security. System Management provides granular control over the community, allowing the admin to suspend or delete accounts, manage the global inventory, and perform database maintenance to clear expired sessions. The Support domain functions as the primary hub for user relations, where the admin manages the ticket queue and provides direct responses to queries. For high-level intelligence, the Oversight section allows for real-time monitoring of all active bookings and comprehensive revenue tracking. Finally, the System Configuration module empowers the admin to maintain platform boundaries by setting limits on user tiers and broadcasting essential System Alerts regarding maintenance or critical updates.
